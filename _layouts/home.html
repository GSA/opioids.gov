Confronting the opioid crisis in the United States

Last year, more Americans died due to opioid overdoses than car crashes. From cities and suburbs to rural America, opioid addiction is the epidemic next door. This website illustrates both the magnitude of the opioid crisis and the actions the Trump Administration is taking to address it.

The Crisis

Drug overdose deaths are now the leading cause of injury death in the United States – outnumbering deaths from car crashes, gun-related deaths, or military fatalities throughout the Vietnam War. The Trump Administration is working to address both drug supply and drug demand to reduce opioid abuse, addiction, and overdose.

Preventing Addiction Through Safer Prescribing

Over-prescribing of opioids has contributed to rising addiction rates. Working with health care providers and patients, the Trump Administration is working to drive down both the number and strength of opioid prescriptions.

Stopping the Flow of Illicit Opioids

Illicit drugs, including opioids, have flooded into our country through ports, shipments, and border crossings. The Trump Administration is undertaking an eort to increase seizures of illicit opioids.

Saving Lives

From providing overdose-reversing drugs to first responders to increasing access to drug addiction treatment, the Trump Administration is working to save the lives of those suering with addiction.

Veterans

America’s veterans have rates of opioid misuse that are higher than the civilian population. Through the Department of Veterans Affairs, the Trump Administration is working to prevent opioid misuse and provide high-quality treatment to veterans suffering from addiction.
